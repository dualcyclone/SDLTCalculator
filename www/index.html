About Stamp Duty in the UK and Scotland

From the 4th December 2014 in the UK, the stamp duty rates have changed. However from 1st April 2015, legislation is due to be passed in Scotland which dissolves Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) in favour of the new Land and Buildings Transation Tax (LBTT).

This site has been produced to help you work out what rates you are likely to pay for your intended purchase price.

Please be aware, only purchases that have exchanged prior to 4th December 2014 can decide which SDLT rates can be applied to their purchase. From 1st April 2015 in Scotland only, the new Land and Building Transaction Tax will be the only tax that applies, and will replace the SDLT that applies in the rest of the UK.

What are the Stamp Duty rates in the UK and Scotland

The Stamp Duty Land Tax rates within the United Kingdom, and the Scottish Land and Building Transaction Tax rates are as follows:

Old UK SDLT rates

This is the old cliff-edge system; the price you pay dictates the rate you pay on the complete purchase price.

This policy is no longer active as of 4th December 2014, but can still be used for purchases where contracts were exchanged prior to this date.

New UK SDLT rates

The new scaled-rate system; The duty is calculated at each band up to the value of the purchase price.

This policy is active as of 4th December 2014. Where purchases have exchanged contracts prior to this date, purchasers have the option to choose which SDLT rate they wish to use.

Scottish LBTT rates

The new scaled-rate system; The duty is calculated at each band up to the value of the purchase price.

This policy will become active in Scotland only from the 1st April 2015. Until then, the above SDLT rates will apply for properties depending on their exchange date.
